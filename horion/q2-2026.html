Insights and recommendations

What stood out in Q2 2026 (April to June), and the creative moves we would make next
Insights
1
Enquiry efficiency
The enquiry campaign opened with 233 messaging enquiries at RM13.20 each across the quarter, the baseline every later month is measured against.
2
Video hook
Of people who started a video across the quarter, 35% reached the 25% mark and 7% watched to the end. Most of the drop off is right at the open, which tells us the first three seconds are not earning attention, the single highest leverage thing to fix on video.
3
Creative concentration
Video 2 alone drove 134 enquiries (58% of the total) at RM8.44 each across the quarter. Leaning this heavily on one creative is efficient today but fragile, the moment it fatigues the whole account feels it, so a ready replacement matters.
4
Who is responding
The 35-44 age band drove the most conversations across the quarter, 74 of 233 (32%), at RM13.53 each, and male audiences accounted for 79% of conversations. Meta is choosing who to serve automatically, so this is a read on who the current creative actually lands with, useful for shaping the next creative's tone and casting rather than for changing targeting.
Recommendations
1
Scale the winner
Keep Video 2 funded and, if anything, give it more room. It brought 134 enquiries at RM8.44 across the quarter, the best cost per enquiry in the set. We'd recommend building two or three close variants of it (same angle, fresh footage or hook) so there is a warm bench ready when it eventually tires.
2
Match format to what works
Video is the stronger format on cost per enquiry across the quarter (RM10.89 versus RM18.34). We'd recommend weighting new production toward video, and reworking the best static concepts into short video.
3
Pause or trim
Video 1 spent RM234 across the quarter for enquiries at RM29.20 each, well above the set average. We'd recommend pausing it or cutting its budget hard and moving that spend behind the proven creative, rather than waiting for it to turn around.
4
Fix the video open
Only 35% of video viewers get past the first quarter across the quarter. We'd recommend re cutting the openings of the running videos to lead with the payoff in the first three seconds, motion, a bold on screen line, or the product in use, rather than a slow brand intro. This is usually the cheapest single change that lifts video efficiency.
