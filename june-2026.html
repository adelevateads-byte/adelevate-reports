Insights & recommendations · June 2026
Insights
1
Cost efficiency
Cost per lead eased to RM10.83 this month, while cost per subscriber fell much further, to RM51.22. The difference comes down to conversion, which climbed to 21.1% from 14.8%, so a larger share of this month's leads are turning into paying subscribers, not just cheaper traffic.
2
Lead channel mix
WhatsApp Messaging remained the main driver this month, contributing 313 leads, 74% of the total. FB / IG DM was the fastest-growing channel, up 45, a sign it's starting to contribute more meaningfully alongside WhatsApp Messaging.
3
Conversion funnel health
21.1% of leads became subscribers this month, up 6.4 points from 14.8%, alongside a 24% increase in lead volume. Both moved in the right direction together, a good sign that the extra leads coming in are converting just as well as before.
4
Spend concentration
Prospecting | Sales Messaging | WA | Agency Content accounted for 35% of this month's spend, with a cost per lead running 10% above the account average. Since it's the largest single campaign, even a modest efficiency gain here would have an outsized effect on the account as a whole.
Recommendations
1
Budget allocation
Prospecting | Sales Messaging | WA | In-House Content produced the cheapest leads this month at RM8.55, compared to RM14.62 for Prospecting | Messaging | WA | CN CONTENT, a meaningful gap between two campaigns on the same platform. We'd recommend shifting some budget from the latter into the former next month and watching whether the cost advantage holds as spend increases.
2
Lead engagement quality
44.4% of the leads reached this month replied back the same day, down from 47.9% in May. A same-day reply is usually a sign of stronger intent, so this dip is worth watching, we'd recommend testing a sharper opening message or narrowing the audience toward higher-intent segments to help bring it back up.
3
Creative refresh vs scale
SmallStudio-CN was this month's strongest performer, with 72 leads at RM5.83 each. 5 of the account's 21 active creatives are each spending under 2% of budget with little to show for it, we'd recommend shifting that spend toward the top performers and retiring or refreshing the underperforming 5.
4
Video content format
Only 47% of 3-second plays reach the 25% watched mark, the steepest drop in the funnel, and just 9% watch to the end. Since most of the loss happens right at the start, we'd recommend prioritising a stronger opening hook on new creative over changes to the middle or end of the video.
